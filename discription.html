Tulsi

Discription

Tulsi is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent and widespread as a cultivated plant throughout the Southeast Asian tropics. It is highly revered in Hinduism and is often grown in homes and temples for its spiritual and medicinal properties.

Types of Tulsi

  • Rama Tulsi (Green Leaf Tulsi): Has bright green leaves and is commonly used in Ayurveda.
  • Krishna Tulsi (Purple Leaf Tulsi): Has dark purple leaves and a stronger taste and aroma.
  • Vana Tulsi (Wild Tulsi): Grows naturally in the wild and has a more subtle aroma.

Habitat

Tulsi grows best in rich, moist soil with ample sunlight. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates but can be grown indoors in pots in temperate regions.
